What we're building

Pets Go is not internal software. It's a market product.

The Brazilian pet industry is massive, growing, and overwhelmingly underserved by technology. The existing software options were built for veterinary clinics — not for pet shops. They're expensive, generic, and require the business to adapt to the software rather than the other way around.

Pets Go is being built specifically for pet shop operations: grooming schedules, WhatsApp automation, loyalty programs, multi-location management. And it's being validated in real conditions — inside a running business with 4 active locations.

The core insight

Junior's business is already big. It's just disorganized.

This is the key insight behind Pets Go: the target customer isn't a struggling startup. It's an established pet shop — already profitable, already busy — that's running on WhatsApp messages, paper notebooks, and gut feel.

Junior's business is the perfect example. Stable revenue, 4 locations, loyal customers. But no visibility into which clients are about to leave, no automated scheduling, no way to see the full picture across locations. The chaos isn't a sign of failure — it's an opportunity.

📋

No customer visibility

Pet shop owners don't know which clients haven't come back in 60 days, which pets are due for grooming, or which customers are their highest-value regulars. Pets Go surfaces all of this automatically.

📱

WhatsApp is the entire operation — and it's unmanageable

Every appointment, every confirmation, every reminder is done manually on WhatsApp by a person. As the business grows, this doesn't scale. Pets Go replaces the manual work with bots that handle it automatically.

🏪

Multi-location with zero central visibility

4 locations, 4 separate operations, no unified view. The owner can't see performance across locations without calling each one. Pets Go gives a single dashboard for the entire network.

Why Pets Go wins

What the existing market doesn't offer

🤖

Real WhatsApp automation — not a workaround

The market leader (SimplesVet) integrates with WhatsApp Web, meaning a human still needs to be at the computer to send messages. Pets Go uses an actual bot: it confirms appointments, notifies owners when their pet is ready, sends vaccine reminders — with zero human involvement. This doesn't exist in the pet shop software market.

🧠

AI-powered customer intelligence

The system identifies inactive customers and triggers re-engagement automatically. It can predict which customers are likely to churn based on visit patterns and prompt action before they're lost. No pet shop software in Brazil does this today.

🔄

Built for multi-location from day one

Most systems treat multi-location as an add-on. Pets Go is being architected for it from the start — separate data per location, consolidated owner view, one login for everything. This is exactly what franchise networks need.

🏗️

Built on the actual operational flow, not a generic template

Pets Go was designed using a detailed product mapping document from Junior — based on how his business actually runs. Every feature solves a real problem in a real pet shop. That's why adoption is high: it fits the workflow instead of fighting it.

The market opportunity

Why the timing is right and why Junior's role matters

Brazil has the second largest pet market in the world, worth over $13.6 billion USD. Over 80,000 pet shops operate across the country. The vast majority are running on manual processes, spreadsheets, or outdated software built for a different type of business.

The pet software market in Brazil is early. The dominant players were built for veterinary clinics and retrofitted for pet shops. There is no product built specifically for pet shop operations with modern automation. That's the gap Pets Go fills.
