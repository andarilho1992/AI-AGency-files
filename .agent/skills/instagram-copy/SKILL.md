# Instagram Copy — Expert Framework

Copy on Instagram is not writing. It's attention engineering. Every word competes
with thousands of other posts for the same second of scroll pause.

---

## How Instagram processes text

The algorithm reads the copy. Not just the image.

- **First 125 characters of the caption** appear without "see more" — they're the only copy most people read
- **Alt text** (filled in manually) is read by the algorithm to classify content
- **Text inside the image/video** is read by OCR — affects distribution
- **Keywords in copy** influence who sees the content (the algorithm has semantic search)

---

## The 4 copy objectives — choose before writing

Every post has a primary objective. The copy changes completely depending on which one:

| Objective | Metric it moves | Copy optimized for |
|----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Reach** | Share to DM + Stories | Content people want to send to someone |
| **Authority** | Save | Content worth keeping to use later |
| **Engagement** | Comment with text | Content that provokes opinion or question |
| **Conversion** | Link click / DM | Content that creates urgency or desire to act |

**Never optimize for all 4 at once.** A post that tries to do everything does nothing well.

## Hooks — The real mechanics

A hook is the first line of the caption AND the main text of slide 1.
They are evaluated in less than 1.5 seconds.

### The 8 hook archetypes that work

**1. Number + specific promise**
> "7 automations any business can have this week"
> "3 mistakes that make your CRM useless"

Rule: the number must be credible. 7 > 10 (10 seems made up). 3 > 5 (denser, more perceived value).

**2. Counter-intuitive statement**
> "You don't need a website to sell online"
> "More followers doesn't mean more clients"

Rule: must be a belief the audience HAS and you're going to challenge. Don't invent empty controversy.

**3. Question that hurts**
> "Why are you still answering WhatsApp manually?"
> "How much time did you waste this week doing what an AI would do in 30 seconds?"

Rule: the pain must be specific and real. Generic question ("Want to grow your business?") stops no one.

**4. Concrete result**
> "This system saved 12h/week for a pet shop in Porto Alegre"
> "From 0 to working CRM in 2 weeks — no code"

Rule: real numbers > estimates. City name or segment increases credibility.

**5. Compressed before/after**
> "Before: 3h answering clients. After: AI does it while you sleep."

Rule: the contrast must be dramatic and immediately visualizable.

**6. Behind-the-scenes reveal**
> "What really happens when you hire an automation agency"
> "Inside a system no client ever sees"

Rule: works because it creates informational FOMO — the person feels they'll discover something others don't know.

**7. Identity provocation**
> "If you're still using spreadsheets to manage clients, this is for you"
> "Business owner with no time: read this"

Rule: the person must recognize themselves immediately. The more specific the segment, the better.

**8. Incomplete statement (cliffhanger)**
> "The biggest mistake pet shop owners make with WhatsApp..."
> "There's a reason your competitors respond faster than you."

Rule: the "..." or "." at the end forces "see more". Use sparingly — loses power if every post.

### What kills a hook

- Starting with "Hi!" / "Hey, how are you?" → sounds like spam
- Starting with the brand name → nobody cares yet
- Generic hook that works for any niche → stops no one specific
- Promise impossible to fulfill in the slides → creates distrust
- Rhetorical question without tension → "Want to succeed?" provokes nothing

## Caption structure by objective

### For REACH (optimized for share)
```
[Hook — provocative statement or number]

[Development in 3-5 short lines]
[Use blank line between each]
[Make each line independent — readable while skimming]

[Final line that invites sharing]
→ "Send this to someone who still does this manually"
→ "Tag someone who needs to read this"

[1 simple CTA]
[3-5 niche-specific hashtags]
```

### For AUTHORITY (optimized for save)
```
[Hook — practical value promise]

[Immediate value delivery — don't stall]
[Compressed list or step-by-step]
[Each item must be actionable on its own]

[Line that reinforces the value of saving]
→ "Save this post — you'll need it later"
→ "Keep this so you don't have to search again"

[Light CTA — no sales pressure]
[3-5 hashtags]
```

### For ENGAGEMENT (optimized for comment)
```
[Hook — opinion-dividing question or divisive statement]

[Context that justifies the question]
[Present two sides if possible]

[Direct question at the end — specific, not generic]
→ "Which tool do you use for this? Comment here"
→ "Agree? Tell me in the comments"
→ Avoid: "What do you think?" — too vague

[No hashtags or very few — doesn't dilute engagement reach]
```

### For CONVERSION (optimized for DM / link)
```
[Hook — specific pain or desired result]

[Problem → escalation → solution]
[Each paragraph increases tension]

[Social proof or concrete result]

[Direct CTA with minimum friction]
→ "DM me 'I WANT' and I'll explain how it works"
→ "Link in bio to see the system live"
→ Avoid: "Get in touch" — too abstract

[No hashtags — hurts conversion by diverting attention]
```

## Carousel copy — slide by slide

Each slide has a different copy function.

### Slide 1 — Visual + textual hook
- Main text: the hook (max. 8 words on slide, rest goes in caption)
- Subtext: context in 1 line (12-14px, secondary)
- Rule: slide 1 and caption must work together — slide stops them, caption convinces them to swipe

### Slide 2 — Problem / Why this matters
- Names the pain with surgical precision
- Language: "you probably..." / "most businesses..." / "the problem is..."
- Avoid: guru language ("you deserve more", "time to change")
- Short copy — 1 strong statement + 1-2 lines of context

### Slide 3 — Turn / Solution
- The most important transition in the carousel
- Structure: [what was wrong] → [what changes] → [how]
- Tone: confident, not arrogant. "There's a better way" > "Only I know how to do this"

### Slides 4-6 — Body / Value delivery
- Each slide = 1 complete idea
- Maximum 3 text elements per slide (title + detail + label)
- Titles: action verbs or concrete nouns
- Descriptions: max. 2 lines, smaller font, secondary color

### Last slide — CTA
- One action only — never two
- Button copy: specific > generic
  - "See system live" > "Learn more"
  - "Send me a DM" > "Get in touch"
  - "Save this post" > "Did you like it?"
- Brand tagline below: short, memorable, on-brand tone

## Bio copy

The bio has 150 characters. It's visited by ~70% of people considering following you.

### Structure that converts

```
Line 1: WHAT YOU DO (specific, not generic)
Line 2: FOR WHOM / RESULT
Line 3: SOCIAL PROOF or DIFFERENTIATOR
Line 4: CTA + link
```

### Examples by tone

**Technical/direct tone:**
```
Digital systems and automation with AI
For businesses that want to scale without hiring more
Operating from anywhere in the world 🌍
↓ See projects
```

**Result tone:**
```
I turn manual processes into automatic systems
Attendance, CRM and reports — no human effort
From Porto Alegre to Thailand and any client in Brazil
↓ How it works
```

**Provocative tone:**
```
Does your business still depend on you to function?
I build systems that work when you don't
Digital nomad | Real projects | No fluff
↓ Talk to me
```

### Bio rules

- **First line** is the most important — appears on Google and Instagram searches
- **Emojis**: max. 2-3, functional (arrow ↓, globe 🌍, check ✓) not decorative
- **CTA line**: always last, always with ↓ or → pointing to the link
- **Avoid**: "passionate about", "I help people", "specialist in" — phrases everyone uses

## Stories copy

Stories have 15 seconds. The copy competes with the finger to advance.

### Story types and ideal copy

**Behind-the-scenes Stories (retains audience)**
- Short, colloquial text, like a friend's photo caption
- "Deploying client system at 11pm" > "Another day of hard work!"
- Shows process, not result

**Quick education Stories**
- 1 fact = 1 Story
- Large font, high contrast, max 10 words per screen
- Series of 3-5 Stories > 1 Story with long text

**CTA / link Stories**
- Copy before the link sticker: creates tension
- "This system has been live since yesterday →" [sticker: See live]
- Don't place the sticker without copy that justifies the click

**Poll / question Stories**
- The question should have an obvious answer for one side (so you understand your audience)
- "Do you already use any automation tool?" [Yes / Not yet]
- Use the result in future posts: "78% of you said you don't use automation. So this post is for you."

## Highlights naming

Highlights are the visual resume of the profile. The name appears below the icon — max. 16 visible characters, but 12 is ideal to avoid cutting.

### Naming rules

- **Short and literal** > creative and confusing
- A new visitor must understand in 0.5s what's in there
- Avoid emojis in the name — takes up valuable space
- Use nouns, not verbs ("Projects" > "What I do")

## Voice tone — Andarilho Digital

Derived from Guilherme's profile + B2B automation niche.

### It IS
- Direct. No fluff, no unnecessary setup
- Technical but accessible — explains without condescending
- Confident without arrogance — proof > assertion
- Pragmatic — talks about results, not dreams
- Human — a real person building real things, not a corporate brand

### It is NOT
- Motivational ("you can!", "believe!", "transform your life")
- Corporate ("innovative solutions", "digital ecosystem")
- Too humble ("trying to help", "still learning")
- Hype ("this will change everything", "the future is here")

### Niche vocabulary — use
automation, system, agent, CRM, flow, integration, scale, process, deploy,
run, configure, connect, save time, no manual effort, 24/7,
while you sleep, without depending on the owner

### Vocabulary — avoid
innovate, disruptive, solution, ecosystem, journey, potential, mindset,
empower, transform (as a motivational verb)

## Pre-publish checklist for any copy

- [ ] Is the hook specific enough to stop someone from the niche?
- [ ] Do the first 125 characters deliver value without needing "see more"?
- [ ] Is there one clear primary objective (reach / authority / engagement / conversion)?
- [ ] Is the CTA a single action, specific, with minimum friction?
- [ ] Is there a niche keyword in the first lines?
- [ ] Is the tone aligned with Andarilho Digital (direct, technical, no hype)?
- [ ] If carousel: do slide 1 and the caption complement each other?
