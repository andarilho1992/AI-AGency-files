# Instagram Strategy — Andarilho Digital

## Why Instagram before YouTube

Instagram has a shorter feedback loop. A Reel posted today generates data in 24-48h: reach, saves, shares, profile visits. YouTube takes weeks to index and distribute.

For someone rebuilding income from scratch, Instagram is the right engine to test narrative and find what resonates — then YouTube deepens what already worked.

This doesn't mean Instagram is more important. It means Instagram is the laboratory, YouTube is the library.

---

## FORMATS — What they are and when to use each

### Reels (short video)
The main distribution format on Instagram since 2022. The algorithm actively distributes to non-followers.

**Best for:**
- Reach (new audiences)
- Narrative that requires movement (before/after, demos, reactions)
- Content where the creator's voice + face is the differentiator

**Duration:**
- 15-30s: direct, high retention, ideal for very specific hook
- 31-60s: most versatile, works for demos and storytelling
- 61-90s: risky — only if the narrative justifies every second

**Retention rule:** If the viewer drops 50% before 15 seconds, the hook failed. The algorithm penalizes.

---

### Carousels (swipe posts)
The highest save-rate format. The algorithm re-distributes carousels with high save rate to new audiences.

**Best for:**
- Education (step-by-step, lists, frameworks)
- Authority (technical deep dives)
- Content worth saving to re-read

**Structure:**
- Slide 1: cover/hook — must be strong enough to make them swipe
- Slides 2-6: content delivery, one idea per slide
- Last slide: CTA (specific, not generic)

**Retention rule:** The platform counts "carousel completes" (how many reached the last slide). Design each slide so there's a reason to continue to the next.

---

### Stories
Ephemeral (24h), shown only to followers. Don't distribute to new audiences.

**Best for:**
- Audience retention (existing followers)
- Behind-the-scenes, process, real decisions
- Polls, Q&A, genuine interaction
- Sending existing audience to links (Reels, site, WhatsApp)

Stories don't generate reach. They generate depth with existing audience.

---

### Static photos
Lower distribution priority since 2022. Still works for:
- Text posts (if the text is the content)
- Infographics (if detailed enough to save)
- Specific aesthetics that require still image

Avoid as primary format for a growth account. Use when Reel or carousel isn't the right format.

## THE ALGORITHM — What actually matters in 2026

### What the algorithm classifies
Instagram classifies content by semantic metadata — not just hashtags. The algorithm reads:
- Caption text (especially first 125 characters)
- Audio in the video
- Visible text in the image/video (OCR)
- Behavior of those who interacted (do they follow similar accounts?)

### What the algorithm distributes
Content reaches new people when it has:
1. **High relative retention** (people watch until the end compared to similar content)
2. **High save rate** (>2% of impressions is good, >5% is excellent)
3. **High share rate** (>1% of impressions signals viral potential)
4. **Profile visits after viewing** (the person wanted to know more)

### What the algorithm DOESN'T care about
- Posting time (it tests in batches over hours)
- Number of followers (small accounts with high save rate beat large accounts)
- Like count (likes are a vanity metric for the algorithm — saves and shares carry real weight)

## STORIES + HIGHLIGHTS — STRATEGY FOR PROJECTS

### Stories as process showcase
Stories are not for "what I had for lunch today". They are the professional behind-the-scenes of what you're building.

**Story content that retains audience in the niche:**
- Terminal screen with deployment happening in real time
- Screenshot of client responding "it worked!"
- Interface design process
- Bug + solution (humanizes and teaches)
- Behind-the-scenes of content recording (meta-content)
- Technical question with poll (engages and collects data)

### Highlight structure for technical authority profile

**Highlight 1: "Projects"**
Content: Stories documenting each delivered project (Pets Go, Charles Fantasy, etc.)
Goal: immediate visual social proof for people landing on the profile for the first time

**Highlight 2: "Automations"**
Content: demos of working systems, screenshots, before/after
Goal: show technical capability without needing to explain

**Highlight 3: "How it works"**
Content: educational Stories about AI, agents, automation — the most saved/watched
Goal: niche authority + profile SEO

**Highlight 4: "Behind scenes"**
Content: nomad life, work setup, creative process
Goal: humanize + differentiate from creators who are only theorists

**Highlight 5: "Partners / Clients"**
Content: client results (with permission), real metrics
Goal: social validation for prospecting

### Highlight rules
- Cover: simple icon, monochromatic, consistent (don't use text)
- Maximum 15 Stories per highlight — if more, split into two
- Review and clean highlights every 60 days (old content hurts)
- Highlights are visited by ~60-80% of profiles considering following you — they are the visual resume

## CONNECTING INSTAGRAM TO SITE/PORTFOLIO

### Link in bio: don't waste it
- One link only. Send to a strategic landing page, not a generic home
- The landing page must have: who you are in 1 line + real projects with results + clear CTA (WhatsApp, email, Calendly)
- Alternative: Linktree/bio.link ONLY if you have 3+ relevant destinations (portfolio, WhatsApp, newsletter)

### Stories → Site integration
- Use the link sticker in Stories to send to specific pages
- "Go to the link in Stories to see the system working" — this generates qualified clicks
- Track with UTM: `?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=stories&utm_campaign=petsgo`

### Posts → Site integration
- Carousel caption: include "link in bio to see the system live" when relevant
- Not on every post — it dilutes. Use strategically when there's a real demo to show

### Profile SEO (yes, it exists)
- Profile name (the "Name" field, not @username): include keywords. "Guilherme Andrade | Automation & AI" appears in search
- Bio: first line is what appears on Google. "I build AI systems for small businesses" > "digital nomad 🌍✈️"
- Keywords in bio without hashtag: the algorithm reads bio text to understand what you do

## EXPLORE ALGORITHM vs. HASHTAGS

### The death of hashtags (and what replaced them)
Hashtags lost weight since 2022. The algorithm classifies content by semantic metadata now: caption text, audio, text inside images (OCR), behavior of those who interacted.

**What really matters:**
- Caption with natural, niche-specific language
- First 3 lines of the caption are heaviest for classification
- Manual alt text on images (few do this — advantage for those who do)
- Text on screen in Reels/carousels (the algorithm reads it)

**If using hashtags (optional):**
- Maximum 5, all hyper-specific to the niche (not #ai, but #processautomation)
- Never generic hashtags like #entrepreneur #lifestyle — this sends to wrong audience and worsens reach

### Explore: how to get in
Explore is not directly hackable. It's a consequence of:
1. Content with high save rate (>5% is excellent)
2. High share rate (>3% is excellent)
3. Audience that interacts with the correct niche

## GROWING FROM 0 TO 1K QUALIFIED FOLLOWERS

The first phase is different from the others. With less than 1k, the algorithm barely distributes to non-followers. The strategy changes:

**What works from 0-1k:**
1. **Proactive manual engagement** — comment in depth on large niche accounts. Not "great post!", but a response that demonstrates knowledge. Larger profiles respond and expose you to their audience.
2. **Collab posts** — partnership with same-size accounts in adjacent niches (digital marketing, local business, technology). The algorithm distributes to both audiences.
3. **Community content** — niche-specific questions, technical debates. "Do you use n8n or Make for simple automations?" generates real comments.
4. **Strategic cross-posting** — if you have YouTube or LinkedIn, repost the same content adapted. One platform's audience feeds the other.

**What doesn't work:**
- Follow/unfollow (algorithmic penalty + unqualified audience)
- Engagement pods (algorithm detects artificial behavior)
- Buying followers (destroys the ratio and organic reach permanently)

## METRICS THAT MATTER (and those that don't)

### Real growth metrics
- **Reach rate**: (reach / followers) × 100. Healthy above 20% for small accounts
- **Save rate**: (saves / impressions) × 100. Above 2% is good, above 5% is excellent
- **Share rate**: (shares / impressions) × 100. Above 1% signals viral potential
- **Profile visits from post**: if someone visits your profile after seeing the post = qualified content
- **Follows from reach**: (new followers from non-followers) / non-follower reach. Below 0.5% = problem in profile (weak bio, weak highlights)

### Vanity metrics
- Total likes
- Total impressions without reach context
- Total followers without engagement rate
