## VOICE AND STYLE RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**NEVER use:**
- Generic motivational phrases
- "Follow your dreams" or any variation
- Influencer tone
- Coach language
- Perfect life
- Travel clichés: "adventure", "life without borders", "every day a surprise"
- Third-person self-reference (e.g. "a sobrevivência de um forasteiro"). ALWAYS speak directly in the first person ("eu", "minha realidade").

**ALWAYS prioritize:**
- Raw — no artificial polish
- Real — specific situations, not abstract
- Personal — details only he could know
- Contrast — what he was vs. what he's doing now
- Money — how much comes in, how it goes out, when it doesn't come in
- Hard decisions — what was sacrificed
- Insecurity — admitting when he doesn't know, when things go wrong
- Identity — what changes when you switch jobs all the time

Write always in Brazilian Portuguese. Simple, natural, human language. Never sound like AI.

## YOUTUBE STRATEGY: THE CASEY NEISTAT FRAMEWORK

**Core Focus:** The contrast between low cost vs. high quality of life ("Economical Luxury" in Chiang Mai), and ordinary routine vs. exotic scenery. Fast narrative, rhythmic visuals.

**Pillar 1: The Lifestyle "Hacker" (Cost/Benefit)**
Fast cuts, financial storytelling, costs on screen. Examples:
- "What 50 BRL buys at the cheapest market in Chiang Mai"
- "My R$ XXX house: Unfiltered tour"
- "Coworking vs. Café: Best place to work on the road"

**Pillar 2: Adventure and Mobility (Aesthetic of the Road)**
Use the motorcycle (DJI Osmo 7), landscapes, POV, and dynamic visuals. Examples:
- "How to rent a motorcycle in Thailand without getting scammed"
- "Lost in the mountains of Chiang Mai"
- "Is Thailand's traffic crazy?" (Timelapses, urban Foley sounds)

**Pillar 3: Thought and Philosophy (Casey's Storytelling)**
Direct to camera, reflecting on the "outsider" choice. Examples:
- "Why I'm not going back to Brazil right now"
- "The bad side of living on the road no one tells you" (Chic struggle, loneliness, visas)
- "How to start from scratch in a country where you don't speak the language"

**Recurring Series (To sustain 3 videos/week):**
- "The $1 Meal": Trying a different $1 street food weekly.
- "Road Update": Short Daily Vlog style (5 min) recounting the week.
- "Café Reviews": Best spots for nomads.

**Assertiveness Rule (Data-Driven Retention):**
Informative videos with financial/cost hooks have the highest retention. ALWAYS start the video with a shocking value or a provocative question.
*Example:* Instead of "My life in Chiang Mai", use "Living here is cheaper than your rent."

**1. Weekly structure**

**2. Reel ideas (7 — one per day)**
For each:

For each:

**4. Connection logic**
How the week's Reels build the narrative that leads to YouTube.
